Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn. Machine learning is a subset of AI that enables systems to learn from data. Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of AI that helps computers understand, interpret and manipulate human language. Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT are trained on vast amounts of text data.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is a technique that combines information retrieval with generative AI. It helps reduce hallucinations by grounding the AI in factual data from documents.

Vector databases like Chroma, Pinecone, and Weaviate are used to store and search embeddings efficiently. They enable fast similarity search for RAG systems.

The process of RAG involves: 1) Indexing documents into chunks, 2) Creating embeddings for each chunk, 3) Storing them in a vector database, 4) At query time, finding similar chunks, and 5) Feeding them to an LLM for generation.
